# Journal

## Purpose

The **Journal** folder contains chronological notes that capture thoughts, observations, activities, experiments, and events as they occur.

Journal entries provide a historical record of the knowledge creation process and serve as a source of future insights.

Unlike Wiki articles, journal entries are intended to reflect ideas at a specific point in time rather than permanent knowledge.

## Typical contents

Examples include:

- Daily notes
- Project progress
- Meeting notes
- Research sessions
- Experiment logs
- Development diaries
- Observations and reflections
- Personal learning notes

## Workflow

Journal entries are captured as a record of events and ideas.

As knowledge matures, important information may be:

- Incorporated into Wiki articles.
- Used to update existing knowledge.
- Referenced by projects.
- Archived once no longer actively relevant.

The original journal entry remains as a historical record.

## What does *not* belong here?

This folder should not contain polished documentation or long-term reference material.

Information intended as permanent knowledge should be developed into Wiki articles.

## Design Principle

Knowledge often begins as a thought.

The Journal preserves that journey, allowing Ariadne to distinguish between ideas as they were first recorded and knowledge as it eventually becomes understood.

---

**In short:**

The **Journal** is Ariadne's notebook, recording the path from first thought to established knowledge.
