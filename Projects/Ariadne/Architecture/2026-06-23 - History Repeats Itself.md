# History Repeats Itself

**Created:** 23 June 2026

## Overview

This image represents one of the major conceptual turning points in the Ariadne project. Rather than focusing on software architecture alone, it places the project into the broader historical evolution of computing.

The central observation is that the AI revolution appears to be following many of the same patterns witnessed during the transition from mainframe computing to personal computers during the late 1970s and early 1980s.

Unlike the architecture diagrams, this document records the historical experience that shaped the project's long-term direction.

---

## Personal Background

This concept was inspired directly by my own career.

I entered the computer industry during the era of large centralised systems, working with IBM mainframes and Digital Equipment Corporation (DEC) systems including the PDP-8, PDP-11 and PDP-15.

Much of my early work involved industrial automation, numerical control systems and custom engineering solutions. One significant project involved helping design and program a dual-axis plotting machine capable of producing highly accurate engineering drawings that were later used to drive optical steel plate cutting systems. Looking back, these machines were early examples of computer-controlled manufacturing that share many concepts with today's CNC machinery and additive manufacturing systems.

Those experiences provided an appreciation for how rapidly computing architectures can evolve while solving fundamentally similar problems.

## From Mainframes to Personal Computers

When personal computers emerged during the early 1980s, many organisations believed they would never replace expensive centralised systems.

History proved otherwise.

Affordable desktop computers eventually performed many of the tasks that previously required specialised hardware costing hundreds of thousands of dollars.

Throughout this period I transitioned from large enterprise computing into consulting for small and medium businesses.

Rather than recommending expensive proprietary systems, I specialised in building practical solutions using affordable PC hardware.

This included:

- designing office networks
- integrating mixed operating systems
- connecting Macintosh, Windows and DEC environments
- installing shared databases
- configuring printers and file servers
- solving interoperability problems that many vendors considered impossible or uneconomical

My value was never simply installing computers.

It was understanding how all of the individual technologies fitted together into reliable systems.

## The Historical Pattern

This image captures a repeating pattern observed over several decades.

Large organisations naturally centralise computing because the technology begins as expensive and highly specialised.

As hardware becomes cheaper and software matures, capability gradually moves outward toward individuals and small organisations.

The transition from mainframes to personal computers followed this path.

The internet followed this path.

Virtualisation followed this path.

Cloud computing partially reversed the trend through economies of scale.

Artificial Intelligence now appears to be beginning another cycle.

Today, frontier AI models require enormous computing infrastructure.

However, smaller and increasingly capable models are rapidly becoming practical on consumer hardware.

The underlying pattern remains remarkably familiar.

---

## Influence on Project Ariadne

This historical perspective significantly influenced the direction of Ariadne.

Originally, the project explored building a large-scale AI Librarian capable of coordinating many cloud-based intelligence providers.

Over time a different question emerged.

Instead of asking how to build the largest possible AI system, I began asking how small businesses and individuals could obtain similar capabilities using affordable local infrastructure.

This represented a major philosophical shift.

The objective became enabling ordinary people to own and control their own AI knowledge systems rather than depending entirely on external providers.

## Personal Reflection

Throughout my career I have repeatedly found myself standing at moments where computing paradigms were changing.

I witnessed the transition from mainframes to personal computers.

I helped businesses navigate networking, interoperability and distributed computing.

Today I believe Artificial Intelligence represents another transition of similar magnitude.

The technologies have changed.

The economics have changed.

The names have changed.

The underlying pattern has not.

That recognition became one of the guiding motivations behind Project Ariadne.

## Legacy

This document records the point at which Ariadne expanded beyond being a software project.

It became a practical vision for helping individuals and small businesses navigate the next major shift in computing.

Rather than competing with large AI providers, Ariadne aims to make their capabilities accessible through intelligent orchestration, local knowledge ownership and affordable infrastructure.

The goal is not to recreate the data centre.

The goal is to do for AI what personal computers did for computing itself: place meaningful capability into the hands of ordinary people.
