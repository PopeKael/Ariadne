# Projects

## Purpose

The **Projects** folder contains collections of work related to specific initiatives, products, research efforts, or long-term activities.

Each project serves as a self-contained workspace where documentation, plans, notes, and supporting material can be organised independently while remaining connected to the wider KnowledgeVault.

Projects often reference Wiki articles, Sources, Journal entries, and Entities, but maintain their own identity and objectives.

## Typical contents

Examples include:

- Software development projects
- Research initiatives
- Creative works
- Business ventures
- Documentation
- Design notes
- Planning material
- Project-specific assets

Each project should be organised within its own subfolder.

## Workflow

Projects evolve over time as ideas become plans, plans become work, and work becomes knowledge.

Throughout a project's lifecycle, Ariadne may:

- Link to relevant Wiki articles.
- Reference people, organisations, and technologies.
- Associate supporting source material.
- Record progress through Journal entries.
- Generate documentation and reports.

Completed projects may eventually be archived while preserving their historical context.

## What does *not* belong here?

This folder should not be used for general knowledge, reference material, or personal notes that are not directly related to a specific project.

Knowledge intended for long-term reuse should be developed within the Wiki.

## Design Principle

Projects represent **work in progress**.

Where the Wiki captures what is known, Projects capture what is being created, explored, or achieved.

Each project remains connected to the wider KnowledgeVault while maintaining its own structure and history.

---

**In short:**

The **Projects** folder is Ariadne's workshop, where ideas become outcomes and knowledge is put into practice.
