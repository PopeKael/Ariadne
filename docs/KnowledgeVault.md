# KnowledgeVault

## Overview

The KnowledgeVault is the permanent knowledge store used by Project Ariadne.

It is a structured collection of Markdown documents that preserves ideas, research, conversations, projects, journals, and reference material in an open, human-readable format.

The KnowledgeVault is intentionally independent of any AI model, application, or software platform. It is designed to outlive the tools that interact with it.

Ariadne is the librarian.

The KnowledgeVault is the library.

---

# Design Philosophy

The KnowledgeVault follows several guiding principles.

## Knowledge is Permanent

Knowledge should remain accessible for decades.

Information must never become locked inside a proprietary database or dependent upon a specific AI provider.

Markdown is the canonical storage format.

---

## Human First

The KnowledgeVault is designed primarily for human understanding.

Artificial intelligence exists to assist with organisation, indexing, retrieval and reasoning.

The human remains the author and final authority.

---

## AI Agnostic

Any AI capable of reading Markdown should be able to interact with the KnowledgeVault.

Current and future AI systems should require minimal adaptation.

The vault belongs to the user, not the AI.

---

## Incremental Growth

The KnowledgeVault is intended to grow continuously.

Every conversation, research session, article, video, project, or discovery should strengthen the knowledge network rather than replace existing knowledge.

The value of the vault increases over time.

---

# Primary Objectives

The KnowledgeVault exists to:

- Preserve knowledge.
- Capture ideas before they are forgotten.
- Organise information into meaningful relationships.
- Record project history and engineering decisions.
- Support lifelong learning.
- Provide trusted context for AI reasoning.
- Build a searchable personal knowledge network.

---

# Current Structure

The initial structure of the KnowledgeVault consists of several logical areas.

## Inbox

Temporary holding area for newly created or imported knowledge awaiting processing.

---

## Sources

Original source material including articles, transcripts, documents and research.

Sources should remain unchanged wherever possible.

---

## Concepts

Distilled ideas and evergreen knowledge extracted from source material.

Concepts become the interconnected "thinking layer" of the vault.

---

## Journal

Personal reflections, observations and conversations.

Journal entries provide long-term continuity and personal context.

---

Additional folders will be introduced as the system evolves.

The organisation of the KnowledgeVault is expected to mature over time without changing its core principles.

---

# Relationship to Ariadne

The KnowledgeVault is not part of Ariadne.

It is an independent knowledge repository.

Ariadne reads, indexes, analyses and organises the KnowledgeVault but never owns it.

If Ariadne is replaced, the KnowledgeVault remains fully usable.

If the KnowledgeVault is moved to another platform, Ariadne should be able to reconnect without modification.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The KnowledgeVault should become a lifelong intellectual archive.

It should allow accumulated knowledge to remain accessible, interconnected and useful regardless of future changes in software, artificial intelligence or computing platforms.

Every addition should strengthen the whole.

The KnowledgeVault is designed to become more valuable with age.
