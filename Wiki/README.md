# Wiki

## Purpose

The **Wiki** is the primary knowledge repository within Project Ariadne.

It contains curated, verified, and interconnected knowledge that has been distilled from journals, projects, source material, research, and other information captured throughout the KnowledgeVault.

Unlike temporary workflow folders, the Wiki is intended to represent trusted, long-term knowledge.

## Typical contents

The Wiki may contain articles covering subjects such as:

- Artificial Intelligence
- Computing and Technology
- Infrastructure
- Networking
- Programming
- Science
- Business
- Personal Knowledge
- Creative Projects
- Reference Material

Knowledge is organised into categories that evolve naturally as the KnowledgeVault grows.

## Workflow

Knowledge typically reaches the Wiki through the following process:

1. Information is captured from one or more sources.
2. Ariadne processes and enriches the material.
3. The information is reviewed and verified.
4. Verified knowledge is incorporated into the Wiki.
5. Related articles are linked to build an interconnected knowledge network.

The Wiki represents the final destination for knowledge that has been validated and refined.

## What does *not* belong here?

The Wiki should not contain:

- Raw source material
- Temporary notes
- Processing artefacts
- System logs
- Drafts awaiting review
- Personal workflow data

These belong within their respective workflow folders until they are ready to become permanent knowledge.

## Design Principle

The Wiki is designed to become a living knowledge base rather than a static collection of documents.

Articles should be:

- Well organised
- Clearly written
- Cross-referenced
- Continuously improved
- Supported by reliable sources

As Ariadne evolves, the Wiki becomes increasingly interconnected, allowing relationships between ideas, technologies, people, projects, and concepts to emerge naturally.

## Repository

The Wiki structure is included in the public repository as part of the KnowledgeVault template.

Each user builds their own collection of knowledge within this framework, allowing Ariadne to provide consistent organisation while preserving individual ownership of content.

---

**In short:**

The **Wiki** is Ariadne's library: the place where captured information becomes trusted, connected, and enduring knowledge.
